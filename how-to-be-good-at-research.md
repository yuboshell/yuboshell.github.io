## Study Note: How to Be Good at Research

<p class="note-meta">A passage on picking a research problem that I keep coming back to — quoted from the essay linked below.</p>

<div class="highlight-para" markdown="1">

the trouble with an absorbed problem is that you hold the conclusion without the reasoning. you know some famous lab cares about a direction, but not why, not what they expect to find, not what would make them drop it. when they pivot, you find out a year later. and on a problem that's already fashionable, you're racing a thousand people who started earlier and have more compute than you.

john schulman's guide to ml research splits the work into two modes. in one, you read the literature and hunt for things to improve. <mark class="key">in the other, you choose an outcome you genuinely want to exist and reason backwards to the experiments.</mark> he argues for the second, and the quiet reason is that it manufactures originality. <mark class="key">a goal you actually care about will drag you into territory no survey paper covers.</mark>

</div>
